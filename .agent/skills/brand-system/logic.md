# PECAÊ Design Logic: The Digital Forge

## 1. UX Psychology: "Value in the Ruins"
Since PECAÊ deals with "sucatas" (scraps), the design must contrast the "decayed" nature of the assets with a "surgical/high-tech" appraisal and marketplace experience.

- **Von Restorff Effect:** Use the Vibrant Electric Green (`#3fff8b`) only for CTAs and critical status (e.g., "Available").
- **Miller's Law:** Chunk vehicle information into technical cards. Don't show long specs lists until "Product Details".
- **Serial Position Effect:** Keep search and price at the very top or bottom (thumb zone) to anchor the user's focus.

## 2. The "Digital Forge" Philosophy
Transforming "scrap" into "premium inventory."

- **Layering Principle:** Treat the UI as a stack of semi-transparent materials. More important info is physically "higher" (more blur, brighter glow).
- **Industrial Premium:** Juxtapose high-end typography (Space Grotesk) against industrial metadata (VIN numbers, tons, mileage).
- **Asymmetry over Boring Grids:** Use staggered card heights for inventory variety, mimicking a real workshop's varied storage.

## 3. Light Mode vs. Dark Mode Strategy
- **Dark Mode (Forge):** Deep Obsidian backgrounds with "powered on" components (ambient glows). High contrast, technical, authoritative.
- **Light Mode (Clean Vidro):** Soft, minty-white glass gradients. Feels like a modern, clean automotive laboratory or showroom. Focuses on "Transparency" and "Recovery" (Green).
